Doctoral Advice & Lifestyle Recommendations

Managing your blood pressure is crucial for cardiovascular health. Below are evidence-based recommendations:

🥗 Healthy Diet

  • Follow a DASH diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and low-fat dairy.
  • Reduce sodium intake to < 2,300 mg/day (or < 1,500 mg/day if hypertensive).
  • Limit processed foods and sugary drinks.

🏃 Physical Activity

  • Engage in at least 150 minutes/week of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise.
  • Include strength training 2–3 times per week.

⚖️ Weight Management

  • Maintain a healthy BMI (18.5–24.9).
  • Even a 5–10% weight reduction can significantly lower blood pressure.

🧘 Stress Management

  • Practice mindfulness, meditation, or yoga regularly.
  • Ensure adequate sleep (7–9 hours per night).

🚭 Avoid Harmful Habits

  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Avoid excessive caffeine if sensitive to blood pressure changes.

📈 Regular Monitoring

  • Track your blood pressure daily or weekly.
  • Consult a healthcare professional if systolic < 90 mmHg or diastolic < 60 mmHg with symptoms.

Note: These recommendations are general and do not replace individualized medical advice. Always consult your physician for personalized care.
